…common question asked

How to get here?

Getting to Havana´s airport. You need to have:

•A valid passport for at least one month beyond your departure date.

•Travel medical insurance (compulsory since May 2010).

•Evidence of enough funds to afford your stay.

•Return air ticket.

•Payment of the airport tax (25 CUC per person) compulsory for every person who leaves Cuba.


Cuban Visas 

If you intend to travel to Cuba for less than two months then you do not need a visa unless you are a business traveller or a journalist in which case you need to apply to the Cuban consulate in your country in advance of your trip. You may instead get a tarjeta de turista ( can be issued upon arrival in Cuba - I personally do not recommend it except in special circumstances) which can be purchased from the travel agency or airlines that sells you the ticket.


When to come?

High season (Nov-April & Jul-Aug) : Prices are higher by 30 %.Hotels and guesthouses may require advance bookings. 

Lower relative humidity and cooler. Cuban winter is like European summer.

Best time (April & Oct) : Lower prices. October belongs to the hurricane season. April is Cuban spring, perfect month to come.

Low season (May, Jun & Sep): Usually rains. Hurricane risk from Jun to Nov. Cheapest prices.


Where to go?

Havana - February holds Havana´s most outstanding festival: The Jazz festival. I personally do not recommend to come over in Cuban summer unless you love the heat! October holds the Festival Internacional de Ballet. Good month to come. December holds the Festival del Nuevo Cine Latino americano.


How do we move in the city and around Cuba?

In Havana some sights are within walking distances, for longer distances Cuba Cruising transportation should be provided.

Where do tours begin and is it possible to add and skip places?

We start your day with delicious breakfast in your hotel or preferred accommodation.

And of course, we can add and skip places, but our tour is always taking care of what you really cannot miss!


Can you make hotel reservations?

Yes, but in our travel expirience is best if we can accommodate you in a Gay Friendly casas particulares that we already carefully selected in Havana and all over Cuba. you can also kindly book your accommodation directly to rental providers or let us know if you need further assistance. We can help follow up and reconfirm your bookings to ensure smooth flow of your stay.


I would like to visit other places of Cuba but I find it hard to arrange transportation and accommodations?

This could be done either before your arrival, or once you're already in Cuba, through a meeting in your hotel or your casa or simply send us your advance request.


What if my straight friend wants to join us?

He/she will be more than welcome!
